Robot

Drive system

For the relic recovery challenge, we use a swerve drive system. Our drive system features 4 “swerve” modules, each of which can independently swivel using a vex motor, and each wheel is driven by neverest motor geared 22.2:1 to the wheel. The wheel we use is a 3d printed dual omni wheel, this way, we can convert between swerve and holonomic mid-match. As Relic Recovery is full of tight spaces, omni directionality is key for this game, and with this drive system, the robot is able to translate itself in any direction, as well as rotate while doing. In order to control all modules, as well as to make sure they do not veer off course, we use vector addition algorithm and PID.

Jewel knocker: 2-point pivot arm

Our robot uses two servos to knock over the jewel. At the base of the robot, a servo with a arm long enough to reach the jewels reaches down in between the jewels. Then, the camera looks at the two jewels, and decides which way to swivel the arm of the robot, which is towards the correct jewel to be knocked off. Then, the second servo at the end of the arm swivels, and knocks the correct jewel.

Harvester

Our harvester features three stages, a vex motor intake, a top harvester, and a second stage harvester. The 2 vex motors use 2 types of surgical tubing to agline angled glyphs. The vex motors are also spring loaded which help with the size variance that glyphs have. Our second stage harvester is tied to the top harvester off one motor. The second stage is used to push the glyphs to the back of the robot with surgical tubing. Our top harvester is used to knock down or pull down stacks of glyphs so we can choose the correct color glyphs for the cipher pattern.

Glyph deposit system: flipper

Our glyph deposit system features two systems, a lift and a flipper. The lift is driven by two neverest motors geared 20:1 with the usage of a orbital gear box. The motors connected to a pulley, which are strung down to the bottom of the flipper. When the driver wants to raise the placement of the flipper, in the event that the driver is scoring in the top two rows, the driver runs these motors. When they want to deposit the glyphs, the glyphs flip into the cryptobox. However, the flipper also has a clamp, which secures onto the glyphs. This way, it is easier for the driver to deposit the glyphs in the upright position.

Relic arm

Our Relic Arm system uses a 2 stage arm, all driven by one motor. Using a neverest motor geared 256:1, an arm extends forward towards the relic. The first arm is kept parallel using a chain, which constantly rotates the arm forward as its rotating around. Then, as this first arm moves forward, the second stage arm rotates using a timing belt to reach the relic. A 3d printed claw at the end grabs the relic, and then rescues it in zone 3, standing.
