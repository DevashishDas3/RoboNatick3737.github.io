Competitions

Maryland Tech Invitational at Atholton High School (June 23-24, 2018)

We saw a lot of good friends at MTI this year, and had a ton of fun competing. Thank you to the organizers for hosting this amazing post-season event yet again!

❯

State Championship at Natick High School (March 3, 2018)

We had a really fun time at States. Since it was at our High School, we helped set up the night before. Competition day did not go as well as we hoped, as our robot disconnected in several matches and autonomous was not as consistent as we had liked. Though we were chosen for the Elimination Round, we lost in the semifinals. We did win the PTC Design Award, however, for our robot's unique build. We were not able to advance to Super-Regionals this season, but hope to attend some post-season events to continue competing!

Vermont Regional Championship at The University of Vermont (February 24, 2018)

We were honored to be invited to this competition in Vermont, and had a great time. Our day started out rough, as autonomous was not as consistent as we would have liked. However, we were chosen by the 1st Alliance Captain, 11115 Gluten Free, to play in the elimination round. Together with Gluten Free and Team 40 HAX Robotics, their 2nd pick, we won the Finals! Although we did not advance to Super-Regionals, we will come back stronger at the MA State Championship.

Qualifier at Arlington High School (February 10, 2018)

The Arlington High School Qualifier was a blast! Though we started out with a tough match, we won the rest of our matches, and placed 3rd overall. As a result, during Alliance Selection, we got to pick our own alliance, pairing up with Team 8227 Enginuity. We advanced to the Finals, where we lost to the 1st Place Alliance. Overall, our robot worked well, though we will certainly make a few tweaks to the build and autonomous to make it more consistent. Finally, we won the Inspire Award, advancing us to the State Championship!

Qualifier at Lexington High School (January 6, 2018)

We had a lot of fun at the Battle of Lexington. Besides a few disconnections, which we will certainly work to fix, our robot performed approximately as expected. In autonomous, it dislodged the correct jewel in most matches. In teleop, we consistently filled up two columns with glyphs. We placed 6th in the first round, were picked by the 2nd place team, and advanced to the finals. Although we lost in the finals, and therefore did not advance to States, we were honored to receive the Motivate Award. With more driver practice and a few tweaks, we are ready to come back stronger at our second qualifier!
